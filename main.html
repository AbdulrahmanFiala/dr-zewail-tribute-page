Dr. Ahmed Zewail

The father of femtochemistry

Dr. Ahmed Zewail while receiving the Nobel prize

Here's a time line of Dr. Zewial's life:

  • 1946 - Born in Damanhour, Egypt.
  • 1967 - Received a Bachelor of Science degree in Chemistry from Alexandria University.
  • 1974 - Completed his PhD at the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1976 - Awarded a faculty appointment at the California Institute of Technology.
  • 1989 - Received the King Faisal International Prize.
  • 1989 - Zewail married his wife Dema Faham.
  • 1993 - Wolf Prize in Chemistry awarded to him by the Wolf Foundation.
  • 1997 - He received the Tolman Award.
  • 1999 - He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
  • 1999 - He received Egypt's highest state honor, the Grand Collar of the Nile.
  • 2006 - Received the Albert Einstein World Award of Science.
  • 2011 - He received the Davy Medal from the Royal Society.
  • 2016 - Zewail died aged 70. Although he was recovering from cancer, the exact cause of death is unknown. A military funeral was held for Zewail on August 7, 2016, at the El-Mosheer Tantawy mosque in Cairo, Egypt.

"When I was a child, I thought of my Delta town as the center of the universe, but now I realize how little I know about the universe."
